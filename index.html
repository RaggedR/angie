Proving Warnaar's Conjecture 2.7

A knowledge-graph-guided strategy map

The conjecture. Warnaar's Conjecture 2.7 (arXiv:2101.01966, $A_2$ Andrews-Gordon identities) asserts the positivity of certain $q$-series coefficients $a^G_{\lambda;r}(q)$ arising from the $A_2$ generalization of the Andrews-Gordon identities. Specifically, the polynomial $a^G_{\lambda;r}(q)$ should have non-negative integer coefficients.

Three Attack Vectors

The knowledge graph reveals three main routes toward a proof, each with different strengths:

① The $A_2$ Bailey Lemma (algebraic, most direct)

The most direct path through the graph. Warnaar's Theorem 1.1 (arXiv:2101.01966) proves the $A_2$ Bailey lemma, which implies Conjecture 2.1, which in turn implies the positivity of $a^G_{\lambda;r}(q)$. Theorem 2.2 specializes to Conjecture 2.8, a close neighbour of 2.7. The foundational machinery is the $A_2$ Bailey tree (arXiv:2303.09069), extending the classical Bailey lattice of Andrews-Schilling-Warnaar (arXiv:math/9807125). The chain is:

$$\text{Thm 1.1} \xrightarrow{\text{implies}} \text{Conj 2.1} \xrightarrow{\text{implies}} a^G_{\lambda;r}(q) \geq 0$$

② Cylindric Partitions (combinatorial)

Exhibit a combinatorial object counted by $a^G_{\lambda;r}(q)$. The knowledge graph traces a 5-hop path from cylindric partitions through Korff's Theorem 1.6 (arXiv:1110.6356) to the Rogers-Ramanujan identities and on to Conjecture 2.8. Corteel-Dousse-Uncu's weighted cylindric partitions (arXiv:1911.13006) may supply the right generating function interpretation.

③ Hall-Littlewood / Schur Positivity (representation-theoretic)

Theorem 1.1 extends Corollaries 3.1–3.4 from Warnaar's 2006 Hall-Littlewood paper (arXiv:math/0607258). Schur positivity connects to LLT polynomials via Assaf's dual equivalence graphs (arXiv:1506.03798), and Littlewood-Richardson coefficients appear directly in Theorem 1.1. This route would prove positivity by expressing $a^G_{\lambda;r}(q)$ in a known positive basis.

Central node: The Rogers-Ramanujan identities are the most connected entity in the graph — every attack vector passes through them. Understanding the $A_2$ generalization of RR is prerequisite to all three approaches.

① The $A_2$ Bailey Lemma

Main Page Algebraic approach — most direct route to Conjecture 2.7

Overview

The classical Bailey lemma produces Rogers-Ramanujan-type identities from Bailey pairs $(\alpha_n, \beta_n)$ satisfying:

$$\beta_n = \sum_{r=0}^{n} \frac{\alpha_r}{(q)_{n-r}(aq)_{n+r}}$$

Warnaar's $A_2$ generalization replaces the single index $n$ with a pair of indices $(n_1, n_2)$ and works with the root system of $\mathfrak{sl}_3$. The $A_2$ Bailey lemma (Theorem 1.1 of warnaar_2021_A2_andrews_gordon) extends the Bailey lattice to this higher-rank setting.

The Chain to Conjecture 2.7

  1. Theorem 1.1 establishes the $A_2$ Bailey lemma, defining $A_2$ Bailey pairs
  2. Conjecture 2.1 uses the $A_2$ Bailey lemma and is equivalent to the Rogers-Ramanujan identities
  3. Conjecture 2.1 implies the positivity of $a^G_{\lambda;r}(q)$ — this is the content of Conjecture 2.7
  4. Theorem 2.2 proves Gordon's theorem and specializes to Conjecture 2.8

What Would a Proof Look Like?

To prove Conjecture 2.7 via this route, one would need to show that the $A_2$ Bailey machinery produces expressions whose $q$-coefficients are manifestly non-negative. Two sub-strategies:

  • Analytic: Find a closed-form or transformation of $a^G_{\lambda;r}(q)$ as a sum of products of $q$-binomials (which are known to be positive)
  • Combinatorial: Interpret the Bailey pair construction as counting lattice paths or partition-like objects, giving a direct combinatorial proof of positivity
Key difficulty. The $A_2$ Bailey lemma involves double sums and $\mathfrak{sl}_3$ weight lattice structure. Positivity is not automatic from the algebraic identity — one needs either cancellation-free expressions or a combinatorial interpretation.

② Cylindric Partitions

Main Page Combinatorial approach — exhibit the objects being counted

Overview

A cylindric partition of profile $c = (c_0, c_1, \dots, c_{n-1})$ is a periodic sequence of partitions $\lambda^{(0)}, \lambda^{(1)}, \dots$ on a cylinder, where consecutive partitions interleave:

$$\lambda^{(i)}_1 \geq \lambda^{(i+1)}_1 \geq \lambda^{(i)}_2 \geq \lambda^{(i+1)}_2 \geq \cdots$$

with the periodicity condition $\lambda^{(i+n)} = \lambda^{(i)} - c$. Their generating functions are intimately connected to the Rogers-Ramanujan identities and their generalizations.

Path to Conjecture 2.7

The knowledge graph traces a 5-hop path:

  1. Cylindric partitions specialize to weighted cylindric partitions (Corteel-Dousse-Uncu)
  2. Theorem 1.6 (Korff) connects cylindric partitions to RR-type identities via the Yang-Baxter equation
  3. Cylindric partitions extend to the Rogers-Ramanujan identities
  4. Theorem 2.2 (Warnaar) specializes to Conjecture 2.8

Why This Could Work

If $a^G_{\lambda;r}(q)$ can be expressed as a generating function over cylindric partitions (or a weighted variant), positivity follows immediately — you are counting objects with non-negative weights. The key challenges:

  • Find the right profile $c$: The $A_2$ setting requires cylindric partitions on a cylinder of circumference related to the $\mathfrak{sl}_3$ weight lattice
  • Match the weights: Corteel-Dousse-Uncu's weighted cylindric partitions already handle general weight functions — can their framework accommodate the $A_2$ specialization?
  • The Korff connection: Korff's work uses the Yang-Baxter equation and quantum groups to connect cylindric partitions to RR identities — the $A_2$ version of this may directly produce Conjecture 2.7
Promising sign. Theorem 1.1 (Warnaar) is directly "about" weighted cylindric partitions in the knowledge graph. This suggests Warnaar himself sees this as a natural combinatorial interpretation.

③ Hall-Littlewood & Schur Positivity

Main Page Representation-theoretic approach — expand in a positive basis

Overview

The Hall-Littlewood polynomials $P_\lambda(x; t)$ interpolate between Schur functions ($t=0$) and monomial symmetric functions ($t=1$):

$$P_\lambda(x; t) = \frac{1}{v_\lambda(t)} \sum_{w \in S_n} w\!\left( x^\lambda \prod_{i < j} \frac{x_i - t x_j}{x_i - x_j}\right)$$

Warnaar's 2006 paper establishes $A_2$ Hall-Littlewood identities (Corollaries 3.1–3.4), which Theorem 1.1 of the 2021 paper extends. If $a^G_{\lambda;r}(q)$ can be expressed as a positive combination of Schur functions or LLT polynomials, positivity follows from known results.

The Schur Positivity Route

Schur positivity means expressing a symmetric function as a non-negative linear combination of Schur functions $s_\lambda$. The knowledge graph links this to LLT polynomials, which are known to be Schur-positive (proved by Grojnowski-Haiman using Kazhdan-Lusztig theory).

What Would a Proof Look Like?

  • Step 1: Express $a^G_{\lambda;r}(q)$ as a specialization of a symmetric function (e.g., as a coefficient in a Hall-Littlewood expansion)
  • Step 2: Show that this symmetric function is Schur-positive, either by:
    • Identifying it as an LLT polynomial (automatic Schur positivity)
    • Using Assaf's dual equivalence graphs to construct a Schur expansion
    • Finding a Littlewood-Richardson rule for the coefficients
  • Step 3: Conclude that $a^G_{\lambda;r}(q) \geq 0$ as a sum of Schur function evaluations
Connection to positivity-preserving $q$-binomials. Warnaar's paper arXiv:math/0505520 studies polynomials $a_l(q)$ and $c_l(q)$ that are related to Rogers-Ramanujan and have positivity properties. These may provide the bridge between the algebraic identities and Schur-positive expressions.

Rogers-Ramanujan Identities

The central node — every attack vector passes through here

The Identities

The first Rogers-Ramanujan identity:

$$\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{q^{n^2}}{(q)_n} = \prod_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{1}{(1-q^{5n+1})(1-q^{5n+4})}$$

The second:

$$\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{q^{n^2+n}}{(q)_n} = \prod_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{1}{(1-q^{5n+2})(1-q^{5n+3})}$$

The $A_2$ generalization replaces the modulus 5 structure with modular conditions related to the root system of $\mathfrak{sl}_3$.

Role in Conjecture 2.7

The Rogers-Ramanujan identities are the most connected node in the entire knowledge graph. Conjecture 2.1 is equivalent to these identities (at the $A_2$ level), and every approach to 2.7 ultimately passes through some form of RR generalization.

Bailey Pairs

The engine of Rogers-Ramanujan-type identities

Definition

A pair of sequences $(\alpha_n, \beta_n)_{n \geq 0}$ is a Bailey pair relative to $a$ if:

$$\beta_n = \sum_{r=0}^{n} \frac{\alpha_r}{(q)_{n-r}(aq)_{n+r}}$$

Given any Bailey pair, the Bailey lemma produces a new Bailey pair, creating an infinite lattice of identities.

The iterative structure underlying Bailey's lemma

Overview

The $A_2$ Bailey lemma extends this lattice to the $\mathfrak{sl}_3$ root system.

Andrews-Gordon Identities

The family of identities that Conjecture 2.7 generalizes

The Identity

The Andrews-Gordon identity (generalizing Rogers-Ramanujan):

$$\sum_{n_1 \geq \cdots \geq n_{k-1} \geq 0} \frac{q^{N_1^2 + \cdots + N_{k-1}^2 + N_i + \cdots + N_{k-1}}}{(q)_{n_1 - n_2} \cdots (q)_{n_{k-2} - n_{k-1}} (q)_{n_{k-1}}} = \prod_{\substack{n=1 \\ n \not\equiv 0, \pm i \pmod{2k+1}}}^{\infty} \frac{1}{1 - q^n}$$

where $N_j = n_j + \cdots + n_{k-1}$. The $A_2$ generalization replaces these with double sums over the $A_2$ weight lattice.

Theta Functions

The analytic backbone connecting modular forms to $q$-series

Overview

Jacobi's theta function:

$$\theta(z; q) = \sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} q^{n^2} z^n = \prod_{n=1}^{\infty}(1-q^{2n})(1+zq^{2n-1})(1+z^{-1}q^{2n-1})$$

Partial theta functions (where the sum runs over $n \geq 0$ only) appear in Warnaar's work on positivity. They lack the modular properties of full theta functions, which makes positivity questions harder.

Littlewood-Richardson Coefficients

Structure constants for Schur function multiplication

Overview

The Littlewood-Richardson coefficients $c_{\mu\nu}^{\lambda}$ appear in:

$$s_\mu \cdot s_\nu = \sum_\lambda c_{\mu\nu}^{\lambda}\, s_\lambda$$

They are non-negative integers (countable by LR tableaux). Their appearance in Warnaar's Theorem 1.1 is significant — it means the $A_2$ Andrews-Gordon identities have built-in representation-theoretic structure.

Schur Positivity

When a symmetric function has non-negative Schur coefficients

Overview

A symmetric function $f$ is Schur-positive if $f = \sum_\lambda a_\lambda s_\lambda$ with all $a_\lambda \geq 0$. This is a strong form of positivity — it implies that $f$ arises as the character of some representation.

LLT Polynomials

Lascoux-Leclerc-Thibon polynomials — known to be Schur-positive

Overview

LLT polynomials are a family of symmetric functions introduced by Lascoux, Leclerc, and Thibon. They are indexed by tuples of skew shapes and are defined via ribbon tableaux. Their Schur positivity was proved by Grojnowski and Haiman using Kazhdan-Lusztig theory.

Assaf's dual equivalence graphs provide a combinatorial framework for understanding LLT Schur positivity via diagonal inversions and descents.

Level-Rank Duality

Swapping level and rank in affine Lie algebra representations

Overview

Level-rank duality exchanges the level $\ell$ and rank $n$ in representations of affine Lie algebras $\widehat{\mathfrak{sl}}_n$ at level $\ell$. For the $A_2$ Andrews-Gordon identities, this duality connects identities at different parameter values, and Theorem 1.1 implies level-rank duality as a consequence.

Involution Principle

Garsia-Milne involution for bijective proofs

Overview

The Garsia-Milne involution principle provides a general method for converting analytic proofs of partition identities into bijective (combinatorial) ones. Applied to Rogers-Ramanujan, it produces explicit bijections between the partition sets on each side of the identity.

For Conjecture 2.7, the involution principle could potentially convert the $A_2$ Bailey lemma proof into a combinatorial proof of positivity.
